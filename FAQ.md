# Open-Pact License v1.1 (OPL-1.1) -- Frequently Asked Questions

## General

### Is this an Open Source license?
Technically, **no**. The Open Source Initiative (OSI) defines "Open Source" as allowing unlimited commercial use and not discriminating against any field of endeavor (like AI). OPL-1.1 has restrictions on Commercial Use and AI Training Use to ensure creators get paid.
We call it "Source-Available" or "Fair Source"—you can see the code and use it for free personally, but business use requires a license.

### How is this different from BSL (Business Source License)?
**BSL** forces you to wait a certain number of years before the code becomes open source. OPL-1.1 **does not**. You can get full commercial rights *right now* by paying a tiered fee. We don't restrict time; we restrict free-riding.

### How is this different from MIT?
**MIT** allows someone to take your code, train an AI on it, replace you, and pay you zero. OPL-1.1 requires those people to obtain a license and pay the Guild.

## For Developers

### I'm a student/hobbyist. Do I need to pay?
**No.** Personal Use is free forever. You can clone, modify, and build anything you want as long as it doesn't generate revenue or train an AI model.

### Can I use this in my internal company tools?
**Yes, for free (Evaluation).** You may use the software for "Internal Evaluation" (testing, prototyping, non-production) to see if it fits your needs. Once it goes into production or supports a revenue-generating service, you need a Commercial License.

## For AI Companies

### Can I train my model on this code?
You can, but you cannot do it for free under a Personal License. You must obtain an **AI Training License**. This fee supports the creators whose code you are extracting value from. Additionally, you must include a **Disclosure** in your Model Card stating that you used this software.

### What if I fine-tune a model and it memorizes the code?
Section 4 requires that if your model outputs code derived from this Software, you must inform your users that the output is subject to OPL-1.1.

## For Projects

### I want to fork this project. Can I?
Yes, under Section 5 (Reciprocity). You can fork the project, but if you make money from the fork, you must share 10% of your royalties with the original Guild.

### What if the project gets abandoned?
We have included a **Stewardship Sunset** (Section 16). If the Guild fails to update the registry or project for 36 months, the restrictions automatically lift, and the software converts to **Apache 2.0** forever.

## Legal

### Can I get in trouble for using it without a license?
If you use it for commercial purposes or AI training without a License Record, you are in violation of copyright. The Guild's registry serves as the public record of who is authorized.
