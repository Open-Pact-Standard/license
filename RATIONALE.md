# Open-Pact License v1.1 (OPL-1.1) -- Rationale

## 1. The Problem: The "AI Tax"

Open source software is built on a social contract: "I give you the code, you give me credit, and maybe some help."

This works great when everyone is an individual developer. It breaks completely when **AI companies** take the entire corpus of open source code to build products that replace the developers, and when **corporations** use the code to make billions without contributing a dime back.

Existing licenses fail to solve this:
*   **MIT/BSD:** Total free-for-all. AI companies love it. Developers get nothing.
*   **GPL:** Great for keeping code open, useless for stopping AI extraction. AI companies argue "training isn't distribution."
*   **BSL/SSPL:** Too restrictive. They kill the "open" spirit by locking the code down for everyone except the owner.

**There is no middle ground. Until now.**

## 2. The Solution: Open-Pact (OPL-1.1)

The Open-Pact License creates a tiered system based on **who you are** and **what you are doing**.

*   **For Humans:** It is open, free, and permissionless. You can learn, fix, build, and share.
*   **For Business:** It is a fair commercial license. You pay a fee scaled to your size, which funds the people who built the tool.
*   **For AI:** It is a specific, higher-value license that requires disclosure and pays a premium for the extractive nature of training.
*   **For Forks:** It ensures that if you build a successful business on our code, you support us.

## 3. Why Not Just Use BSL?

**Business Source License (BSL)** requires a time-delayed release to open source. It forces a "wait 4 years" rule.
*   **Criticism:** It turns open source into a marketing trick. "Look at our code! You just can't touch it for a few years."

**OPL-1.1** is different.
*   **Transparency:** We are transparent from day one. No black boxes.
*   **Fairness:** We charge for extraction, not for access. We don't force you to open-source your whole company (like SSPL).
*   **Governance:** The "Guild" ensures the community owns the project, not just the CEO of the company that started it.

## 4. Why Guilds?

In traditional open source, maintainers are often burnout-prone volunteers.
OPL-1.1 introduces the **Guild**: a decentralized, on-chain body of contributors that:
*   **Manages Treasuries:** Royalties go to a shared pot, not just one person.
*   **Votes on Policy:** Fees, exceptions, and enforcement are decided by the community.
*   **Enforces Licenses:** Uses the blockchain as a public ledger of compliance.

## 5. AI-Specific Protections

Section 4 is the crown jewel of OPL-1.1.
*   **Disclosure:** Forcing AI companies to list their training data in their "Model Cards." This creates public pressure and traceability.
*   **Anti-Competitive:** Preventing an AI company from training a model specifically to replace the tool it was trained on.
